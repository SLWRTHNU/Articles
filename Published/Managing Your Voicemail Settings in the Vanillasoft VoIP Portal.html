The Vanillasoft VoIP portal gives you full control over your voicemail experience, allowing you to record a personalized name, a standard greeting, and an out-of-office message for your extension. You can also configure how you receive email notifications, enable auto transcription, and manage stored messages directly from the portal.

Keeping your voicemail settings current ensures callers have a professional experience when they reach your mailbox, and that important messages are never missed. This article walks you through configuring voicemail recordings, adjusting advanced options, and managing your voicemail messages.

Configure Voicemail Recordings

  1. Log in to the Vanillasoft VoIP portal.

    • Note: This portal may display Cloudli branding because it is hosted by Cloudli. All configuration and support are handled by Vanillasoft. For assistance, contact Vanillasoft Support.

  2. Open the Telephony menu from the main navigation.

  3. Select the Voicemail submenu.

  4. Click the extension you want to configure.

  5. Click Edit.

  6. Click the record button next to Name to record your full name.

    • Your name recording is played to callers to identify the voicemail box they have reached.

  7. (Optional) Click the record button next to Greeting to record a voicemail greeting for callers.

    • This greeting plays when callers reach your voicemail.

  8. (Optional) Click the record button next to Out of Office to record an out-of-office message.

    • This message temporarily overrides your standard greeting when you are away.

  9. When your recordings are complete, click OK to confirm.

  10. Click Save to apply your changes.

Configure Advanced Voicemail Options

  1. Log in to the Vanillasoft VoIP portal.

    • Note: This portal may display Cloudli branding because it is hosted by Cloudli. All configuration and support are handled by Vanillasoft. For assistance, contact Vanillasoft Support.

  2. Open the Telephony menu from the main navigation.

  3. Select the Voicemail submenu.

  4. Click the extension you want to configure.

  5. Click Edit.

  6. Click the Advanced Options tab.

  7. Click Edit to enable editing on this tab.

  8. Configure the available options as needed.

    • Email Notifications – Enable or disable email alerts when a new voicemail is received.

    • Auto Delete After Email – Automatically remove the voicemail message after the notification email is sent.

    • CC on Voicemails – Select another user to receive a copy of voicemail notifications.

    • Auto Transcription – Automatically transcribe voicemail messages to text.

    • Force Password Change – Require the user to reset their voicemail PIN on next access.

    • Callback Number – Select the number the extension will use when returning calls.

  9. Click Save to apply your changes.

View and Manage Voicemail Messages

  1. Log in to the Vanillasoft VoIP portal.

    • Note: This portal may display Cloudli branding because it is hosted by Cloudli. All configuration and support are handled by Vanillasoft. For assistance, contact Vanillasoft Support.

  2. Open the Telephony menu from the main navigation.

  3. Select the Voicemail submenu.

  4. Click the extension you want to manage.

  5. Click Edit.

  6. Click the Messages tab to view all voicemail messages for the extension.

  7. Select the messages you want to remove, then click Delete.

  8. When prompted, click Yes to confirm the deletion or No to cancel.

  9. (Optional) Click the Notifications tab to view any voicemail-related notifications for the extension.

Your voicemail is now fully configured and ready to receive messages. With your greetings recorded, advanced preferences set, and message management in place, your mailbox will handle incoming messages professionally and keep you informed the moment something arrives.
